## Running Inner Light

Inner Light is a module for Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition, intended for a party of four level 3 characters. This setting is intended for use in space-faring fantasy settings, such as Spelljammer, but this module may also be inserted into any setting. This adventure is intended to be played over a single 3-4 hour session of play.

This adventure is a more linear adventure, meant to funnel players from their starting point at their ship, through the jungles of Lux, towards the city core, and into the city's command center. The adventure follows the party helping the abandoned spirit of an elf named Veri, who wishes to reactivate their dead city and the constructs that populate it. As the players explore they will learn about the fate that befell the city in the process. Certain details have been left intentionally vague, allowing this adventure to be more easily slipped into a variety of official Dungeons & Dragons and homebrew settings.

### Ferrulynne

Ferrulynne is a rust-colored mineral that the people of Planet Lux use as a catch-all crafting material. It is mined from drained ground water reservoirs that become taken over by the mineral over millennia. Ferrulynne  is a natural battery for storing magic and excels as a lens to project light. It is sturdy and can also be crafted into weapons, armor, and building material.

All constructs and magic items made with Ferrulynne work by an intricate network of sharp cracks and angles formed within the mineral structure, used to control the flow of arcane energy. The specific formation of these inner cracks dictates the function of the magic item. A player may recognize how this technique functions with a DC 18 Arcana check, but will not be able to reverse engineer these items or create new ones without further long-term study.

### The Planet Lux & its History

![Planet Lux Splash Art](/pages/adventures/inner-light/lux.png "Planet Lux Splash Art")

Lux is a humid planet with vast oceans and deep reservoirs of ground water. These reservoirs sometimes empty and may, over millennia, fill with a dense rust-colored mineral. Vegetation grows fiercely and dense on Lux, covering almost all available landmass with a jungle of thick vines and trees. Fauna on this planet has evolved to adapt to the dense jungles, sporting long limbs for climbing and sharp talons to hack at the dense vegetation. Overhead, colossal insects prowl the skies hunting for creatures that peak through the tops of the dense jungle canopy.

Several thousand years ago, a colony of elves settled the planet, clearing out a hub for themselves in the vast jungles. Rather than carve through the jungles further, they instead focused on refining their existing colony into a great city. Over a millennia, these elves mastered their craft of the rust-colored mineral, dubbing it Ferrulynne. They crafted it into colossal towers and works of artifice. They also used it to hone their spellcraft, as they had discovered it contained an innate property to store Evocation and Illusion magic.

Nearing the end of their long elven lives, the people of the colony refused to pass on. They were unsatisfied with their progress in crafting their city and would not trust the next generation to continue their work. They were untrusting of those born on the planet itself, believing that they lacked the grit of the original colonists. Instead of passing on, they used their knowledge of Ferrulynne to bind their souls to constructed bodies. Their leaders chose to meld with the very buildings of the city itself, taking control of the city's operation from their younger offspring.

Thus, without much independence themselves, the people of Lux were not prepared for invasion. Insectoid creatures landed their ships on the city's borders and slaughtered all within, taking with them the blueprints to perform the same soul-preserving magic that the city elders had used. The city was left to ruin. Weeping for their lost home, the elders ordered all constructs, and the city itself, to shut down to avoid future detection. Only one construct disobeyed, firing off a distress beacon before ejecting their soul from their constructed body. There it remained for thousands of years.

## Introduction

> Violet light washes over the polished mahogany deck of your airship, casting it in an ethereal glow, reflected from the three small moons that orbit the planet below. Strapped into your seats below deck, the ship plunges into sharp dive. Its hull thrashes against the thick atmosphere and the illusory display in front of your seats floods with blinding yellow flame. The vessel slows and the din breaks into a serene calm. The flames in the illusory displays are replaced with a lush verdant landscape, vegetation thriving in the midst of ruins made from broken rust-colored crystalline towers. The planets three violet moons shine faintly against a sky of pinks and blues that swirl like a painted tapestry overhead.

The voice over the intercom is in Elvish and says, "It is now safe to depart. Enjoy your stay on planet Lux."

If they have not done so yet, take this moment to have the characters introduce themselves as they complete their landing.

> Exiting the ship, you enter a wide clearing in the lush jungle. Over the horizon the tips of rust-colored towers peak over the treetops. You have landed on an overgrown dirt path crisscrossed with vines. Floating ahead on the path is a floating purple orb of flame with a bright yellow core, flickering like candlelight. Off to the side of the path is a wrecked mass of a destroyed construct, made from that same rust-colored mineral that makes up the towers.

## Mission & Setup

The party has been summoned to the planet Lux in response to a faint distress signal from a far away world. Their reason for pursuing the distress signal is their own. They may be explorers, salvagers, archaeologists, or perhaps in need of fuel or ship repairs. Regardless of their origin, their primary goal is to seek the source of the distress beacon before leaving the planet. The ship was instructed to land within sight of the beacon.

The player's ship has no other information on the planet Lux, save for its location and its three violet moons. In reality this planet has been in a state of hiding for over four thousand years and most information on its existence has been lost to time. A player may make a DC 15 Nature or History check, confirming the dearth of information on this planet on a success. If a player makes a DC 20 History check, they can confirm that a great battle occurred on the planet, wiping out all life, and little else is known.

The distress signal is a code resembling Morse Code. This code can be cracked with a DC 15 Intelligence check, followed by a DC 12 History check. The language is an ancient dialect of Celestial. It repeats the same message, "They are here. Send help.". The ship's main console has a receiver for the distress beacon and it can be listened to there. A player may make a DC 12 Tinkerer's Tools check or a DC 15 Sleight of Hand check to remove the receiver from the console and take it with them. The description for the Portable Receiver item is listed in the "Items" section later in this module.

## First Steps onto Planet Lux

The players exit their vessel three miles from the city's core. This clearing is a pathway that leads from the city to a distant mineral mine ten miles further in the jungle. This clearing was the site of a minor battle. Thousands of years prior to this adventure, the construct that patrolled this path lay waste to one of the insectoid soldiers that invaded their city. In the process, the construct was also destroyed, firing their distress beacon and exiting their construct body before it shut down.

The orb of purple and yellow flame is **Veri – Abandoned Soul**. Veri will try to make contact with the party and lead them down the path towards the city core. If the players ask for reward, Veri attempts to communicate some form of compensation. They are agreeable to handing over technology, providing a place of rest, or anything they can reasonably offer to help the party.

Nearby Veri is the heap of their old construct body, crafted from several concentric rings lined with razor sharp spikes and inlaid with rubies. It is also the source of the distress beacon. The construct body was once a flying drone, made from the common rust-colored mineral, Ferrulynne. The construct can be harvested for 5 *Ferrulynne Nuggets* and 150gp worth of gems. A DC 15 Arcana or Investigation check reveals this construct to be a scout that is capable of housing an undead spectral creature. The mechanism to house the undead appears at first glance to be broken beyond repair. It however can be repaired with a DC 20 Tinkerer's Tools check, after which the construct becomes a deactivated **Crystal Scout**. Veri will not enter the construct willingly and will keep ejecting themselves from the construct body if they are forced inside it. If Veri is forced in the body, a outbound signal reveals the position of Veri and the party to the city's inhabitants.

Also nearby is the corpse of a humanoid creature with mantis-like features. They are wearing *Plate Armor* and holding a *Scimitar*. The creature is slashed with deep wounds and scorch marks dot its carapace. A DC 12 Medicine check reveals that this body is thousands of years old, as the carapace appears to have undergone a similar process to calcification.

### Roleplaying Veri

Veri was once a founding elf of the colony of Lux. They are hopeful and reject the dogma of their elders, believing in their duty to turn the deactivated city back on and keep living without fear of future invasion. They are an optimistic and tenacious soul with a drive to be helpful to their companions. In removing themselves from their construct body, they were cut off from all communication with the city, meaning that they have no insight to how severe or long lasting the damage from the invasion was.

Veri cannot speak in this state. They can only communicate through broadcasting their emotions and through dimming/shining their light. When held close, Veri gives off a warming heat and a soft pale lavender glow. They appear as a single purple flame with a bright yellow core, small enough to fit in the palm of one's hand.

## Through the Jungle

![Talon Toad Splash Art](/pages/adventures/inner-light/talon-toad.png "Talon Toad Splash Art")

The path towards the city core is considered Difficult Terrain, as it has been overgrown with vegetation and been partially reclaimed by the wilds. It takes two hours to reach the city's core when traveling at a Normal Pace.

The last vestiges of the colony's magic is what keeps the path from being reclaimed entirely. The edges of the path glow with the signs of Abjuration and Necrotic magic when viewed using the *Detect Magic* spell. This magic previously blocked and killed overgrowth, but has faded significantly over time.

## Into the City Core

> Skeletons of elves and calcified carapace flood the streets, laying at rest among the wreckage of crystalline drones and humanoid constructs. The pathway turns from dirt to a mosaic of blue cobblestone tiles. Crystal towers flank the path, lit brightly by flickering illusory billboards marked with Celestial characters and pink skinned elves with pitch black eyes. The cobblestone pathway continues to a courtyard, the center of which sports a large dome made from a rainbow of melded gemstones – each gem glistening in the afternoon light. Veri brightens and blinks rapidly. You feel a tug on your emotions: excitement, loss, mourning, and hope.

A player may make a DC 13 Insight check, learning that Veri is feeling conflicting emotions on a success. They have been away from their home for so long, but have returned to see their companions dead, deactivated, and their hard work in building the city in ruin. Yet, there is a hope that the damage could be reversed.

Unless previously defeated, 2 **Talon Toads** lurk in the crystal towers that flank the path. Have each toad make a Stealth check, contested against the players's passive Perception. They will ambush the party upon crossing their line of sight, each one using their Tongue Lash action as part of a Surprise Round.

Each of the towers can be inspected, but each one has been ruined. Creatures may succeed a DC 12 Investigation check, learning that the attack on the city was sudden and quick on a success. It appears that the day of the attack had been a normal day, until the sudden invasion. On a DC 15 Investigation check, players may learn that the invaders were technologically inferior and swarmed the city with massive numbers to compensate.

## The Command Center

Veri will continue to pull the players towards the Command Center. 

> Spectral elves, one of each color of the nine gemstones, float in a circle above a tower of gemstones. Their bodies are tethered to the tower by a thin thread of pale light. They do not appear to speak, but their thoughts are projected as Common. "No, we cannot fight them. Shut it all down. We will retreat into the towers and build our new lives amidst the ethereal space between realms. We have done much for our children, but they will die so that we may live on. If we remain quiet, the invaders may not return."

The Command Center is crafted from magically melting a variety of nine different multi-colored gemstones together into a single dome: rubies, sapphires, emeralds, amethysts, diamonds, opal, topaz, citrine, and tourmaline make up the gems. A block of Ferrulynne, inlaid with a sapphire gem, is extruded from the left-hand side of the two doors. This panel creates a *Wall of Force* spell and bars entry into the dome, inside of which is a single descending stairwell. This effect can be dispelled with the use of a *Dispel Magic* spell. Making contact with the sapphire gem causes an illusory elf to be projected in front of the doors. It is capable of limited communication, but does not have sentience. This illusion knows the following:

- The city remains in a state of emergency and all residents have been ordered to seek shelter.
- The dome has been locked down and is off limits for all but high-ranking officials.
- Residents should trust that "The Nine" will solve this emergency and all will be well.

The illusion can be fooled with a DC 15 Performance check to impersonate a high-ranking official. The illusion can also be persuaded to ignore orders with a DC 18 Persuasion check. Alternatively, a player may expend 10 *Ferrulynne Nuggets* to make a DC 12 Tinkerer's Tools or Sleight of Hand check, to disrupt the flow of arcane energy in the panel, breaking it. This would disable the illusion and the *Wall of Force* spell.

### The Hall of Memories

The stairwell descends to a 30ft. x 30ft. room. At the center of the room is a 10ft x 10ft cube of Ferrulynne riddled with inner cracks that form a weave of sharp right angles and bends. The front face sports 9 gems, the same nine that made up the dome: rubies, sapphires, emeralds, amethysts, diamonds, opal, topaz, citrine, tourmaline. On the far end of the chamber is an elevator that descends down to a basement level.

This cube stores all memories from the construct residents of Lux. Touching the colors in a specific sequence allows for Veri to channel an illusion of that memory in the chamber for viewing. Most memories will be of the battle, patrols, or simple day to day life on Lux. 

Pressing each color in sequence displays the last memory captured of "The Nine", the city elders and original colonists of the planet.

> Spectral elves, one of each color of the nine gemstones, float in a circle above a tower of gemstones. Their bodies are tethered to the tower by a thin thread of pale light. They do not appear to speak, but their thoughts are projected as Common. "No, we cannot fight them. Shut it all down. We will retreat into the towers and build our new lives amidst the ethereal space between realms. We have done much for our children, but they will die so that we may live on. If we remain quiet, the invaders may not return."

### The Seats of the Nine

Descending down the elevator brings the players to the seats of The Nine.

> A towering column of multi-colored gems extrudes up from the granite floor, 20ft. high. A number of these gems are cracked, the shards of these gemstones littering the floor like a rainbow of broken glass. The tower glows and hums, flooding the air with a thrumming bass. A deep voice rings in your heads, "Why have you come, lost ones?"

Over the millennia, the nine elders have melded into a single self-conflicting personality. Their emotions are erratic and their voices layer atop of one another. They see the party as a direct threat to their secrecy, but feel sad at the understanding that they must die to preserve that secrecy. The ancestors believe it is better for the souls of the city to remain in status, rather than to be eradicated by the invaders.

**The Nine** cannot be reasoned with and will turn hostile after a short conversation with the players. The exception would be nine consecutive DC 18 Persuasion checks, convincing each personality in turn. A single failure pivots the entity back towards hostility towards the party. 

> The lights dim and five crystalline entities, each one sporting combinations of the colored gems, extrudes from the floor – one humanoid being and four sets of concentric floating rings,  A storm of electricity and flame surges within the tower, spouting sparks and heat from its cracks.

1 **Crystal Soldier** and 4 **Crystal Scouts** assault the party. The soldier will attempt to defend at least one of the scouts at any given moment.

### Fighting The Nine - Phase Two

When the constructs are destroyed, the storm within the tower rushes from the cracks. It forms a serpentine surge of rainbow light that assaults the party. This is **The Nine**, their elvish spectral bodies long since having merged into a single entity. On initiative 20, the lair casts the *Entangle* spell, taking the form of brittle gem vines that snake up from the ground. The spell lasts only one turn before it dismisses itself at the next start of initiative 20.

## Conclusion

With The Nine defeated, the Commander Center is available for command. If allowed, Veri will inhabit the Command Center and restore power to the city. The various constructs that fill the streets will reactivate, restoring life the city. Veri will also gain the ability to communicate to the players with actual speech, speaking in Common through the tower.

Veri will reward the players each with 100gp worth of gems, as well as three random *Gem of Cantrips*.

## Items

### Ferrulynne Nugget

*Wondrous Item, Uncommon*

A piece of Ferrulynne Nugget may be used as crafting material to repair or damage other magic items made from Ferrulynne. Refer to the description of that item for any checks to use this item as a material in repair.

This item may be used to store the single use of a level 1, or lower, Evocation or Illusion spell at the expense of a Spell Slot. This spell can then be cast, while holding this item, without the use of a Spell Slot by any creature at the expense of an Action and without the use of Verbal, Somatic, or Material components. The spell is cast at Instantaneous speed.

### Ferrulynne Shield

*Armor (Shield), Rare*

While holding this shield, if you are hit and receive damage by an Attack Roll, as a Reaction you may reduce that damage by 1d4 and you may deal that much Force damage to a creature within 5ft.

### Portable Receiver

*Wondrous Item, Rare*

This item intercepts all communication either dispatched or received by a creature within a mile radius of this item. The communication must have originated from a Message or Sending spell. The communication is represented by a Morse Code representation of the language, which can be understood with a DC 15 Intelligence check upon first learning the code.
