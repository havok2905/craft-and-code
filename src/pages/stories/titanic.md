> Time travel tourism to the Titanic, with a game show to see if you can stop it from sinking

All expenses were paid they told us, well presuming you lived long enough to see it all. You would have believed that, to a Time Traveler, time was a limitless commodity. But, time, my friends, is all relative. Eons become instant in the face of your own mortality. 

I realized this when the ship’s bow tipped upwards to the sky. Roughly six thousand years from now some chicken-shit teenagers will be watching a satellite view of my crotch as piss flowed down my left pant leg. Welcome to Survivor; Season 12,521 baby, though the show hardly resembles its humble island origins these days. Long gone are the days of wacky games and the drama of voting someone “off the island”. The show had grown far simpler. There was one rule – survive, regardless of where or when we put you! Fuck, that water looks really fucking cold.

I sprinted up the bow of the ship, though that got harder with each passing second. I of course knew the fate that waited us all, that was the point. It wouldn’t be half as entertaining to watch otherwise.

Above the ship, a luminous clock ticked and the sound reverberated in my head. Already? I looked up to see the ghastly green phantom numbers tick off the seconds remaining. Fifty eight seconds to run and find holding. But, that was an eternity to these white-collar trained office job legs. I screamed as the ship lurched and I blindly reached out for whatever I could and found a rail. There was a sickening crack and the ship cleaved the night in two.

My fingers slipped and I fell backwards. I could see the numbers tick down overhead. They shined against the canopy of stars that blanketed the night sky. Two seconds remained.

My back broke against the water and I felt the sting of ice and shock rocket through my bones, well my upper half at least. I had lost feeling below the waist at that moment. But then, there was warmth, cheers, and confetti rained down on my face from the rafters as I writhed on stage in agony. Tears welled in my eyes.

I had survived.
