> A world where everyone wheres a different kind of bell as an identifying feature

Allania strode forward in the blackness. It was all encompassing, a smothering veil upon which no mortal could see through. But, it was not sight that guided the young woman, rather it was the tinkle of the tiny bells on her many brass rings. The chimes reverberated out in a wave. They reflected back at her and illuminated every groove of etched into the metal tiles beneath her feet, every craggy wrinkle in the bark of the great oaks that flanked the path, as well as the figure that stood unmoving several yards ahead of her.

Allania froze and chimed the bells on her fingers once more, a bright regal chime – a warning and declaration of her station. The figured had not budged.

“It is customary”, Allania said, “to respond with one’s own chime.”

The figure made no sound, so Allania chimed her bells once more to illuminate the scene to ears. The figure had suddenly moved within arm’s length of her, yet made no sound on the metal path as it ran.

Allania panicked and her heart beat in her chest. She could hear the faint rush of blood speeding through her veins at breakneck speed as the adrenaline took over. The dagger at her hip had never felt so present clasped to her hip. She finally heard the creature’s breathing. It was a harsh gravelly tone, one that wheezed with every ragged breath. Allania drew the dagger from it’s sheath.

“By the Shades, step back or I will draw blood foul creature!”, Allania said.

She lunged, knuckled white in a death grip around the hilt. Warm blood flowed down the blade, down the hilt, and dripped from her fingers, clanging on the metal tiles beneath her feet. The breathing did not stop. Allania screamed and ran. She spun on her heels and bolted blindly behind her, not bothering to sound off her chimes in fear that the creature would follow her.

There were several voices now. They all emerged from the treelines – ragged, coarse, and oozing with the same vitriol as her previous assailant. She fell to her knees.

The forest was overtaken by a din of screams, the tinkling of bells, and then there was only silence.
