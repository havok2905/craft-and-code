> Magical lights that, acting as bits, together make up a computer.

Dr. Polinea Merieadas, or just Polly for short ( thank you very much ), hung her thick wool coat on the wooden rack at the door and slumped at her desk, head held solemnly in her hands. She had begun to lose hope in her experiments as of late – it had been weeks with no results – yet she dragged herself across campus to work regardless. The stark truth of it all was that she had nowhere else to go. Braving the ice storms and hail that rendered the campus grounds a metaphorical warzone was far preferable than admitting she had been wrong, that the isolation and ostracization from her colleagues had been all for naught.

She ran her fingers through her pale white hair, a customary color for scholars of the University. Though, she had three thin streaks of teal that she kept tucked behind her ear. Well, if she was going to be treated an outsider, she may as well look the part she thought. She kept the rest of her hair tied back in a long tail, keeping it out of the way of the pair of bronze rimmed spectacles that she wore. 

The lenses were made of a smoky kind of quartz that was only just translucent, yet easy enough to see through when cut this microscopically thin. The frame itself held a negative Essence charge which absorbed the omnipresent light of the Physical Realm’s Essence. This, in concert with the unique properties of the lenses, allowed her to peer into a star filled vista in the beyond. Motes of dazzling light sparked in and out of view at random patterns, and flickered at just as random frequencies when observed. Her colleagues had informed her that her hunt was a folly, but Polly was certain there was a was a cause-and-effect relationship to those lights.

Polly got to work. She fixated her gaze through the lenses and at the dazzling lights that had coalesced at her desk. She scratched her ink tipped quill at the fresh page of her ledger, flooding the page with the hatch-like glyphs of her shorthand. Hours passed, and like every day, she could form no connection between the rhythm of the light’s flicker, nor their positions. Just when she thought she had noticed a pattern, the light’s variables would twist and weave as she herself had pivoted her thoughts. 

Having spent another day in failure, Polly’s thoughts now pivoted to that dark place in the far recess of her mind that she dared not revisit. Her hands trembled and her breath quickened. The corners of her vision blurred. No. Polly steadied her hand and began to count the lights, an old trick she fell back on to calm her thoughts and to ground her back to reality. Then she saw it.

Polly froze and she felt a chill rush through her veins. She could have sworn that the lights just blinked in rhythm with her counting. It hadn’t been all of them, but rather a small cluster at the center of her vision. They appeared to form a small spiral, with lights randomly blinking on and off as she counted. She stopped and began again. Sure enough, the same sequence repeated and in the same exact order as before. Polly abandoned her writing and drew furiously in her ledger, detailing the position of each and every mote of light, filling page after page as she counted.

The howling winds and hail pelted the roof of her study and filled the air with an ominous din as she silently worked at her desk, careful to blink as little as possible. A single bead of sweat dripped from her brow as she, at long last, stopped. She studied the pages and let out a loud gasp. They were numbers, not numbers she was used to of course. Rather, they were a kind of number system she had studied in her mathematics and logic coursework as an undergraduate. The lights were performing binary math. She had identified a single swirl of lights that acted as a memory of her counted off numbers. This grew with each count, appending a new mote of light to the tail of the swirl, as lights dimmed on or off to represent the binary sequence. The lights had been counting, but could they do other things?

The answer to Polly’s question was a resounding yes. Polly launched herself at the bookcase at the far end of the room and rummaged through tomes she hadn’t dusted off in close to a decade. She poured through her old coursework and filled her ledger with increasingly complex equations and took note of the dazzling lights through her spectacles.

Polly’s findings would not be accepted until long after her death. While mocked in life, “Polly’s Folly” would go down as the first major discovery of other realms, one that would be referred to as The Source; the realm through which all souls compute and where all memories and experiences are integrated into upon our deaths. While Polly’s findings only observed her own thoughts in concert, others would leverage this machinery to their own ends, and jettison humanity to new heights and new lows.
